Choose your Internet speed

 Help me choose

Help me choose

Some people pay a premium for 25 Mbps High-Speed Internet and use every bit of it. Others pay for 10 Mbps but honestly would be just as happy with their speed at 3 Mbps; actually, they would be a lot happier with 3 Mbps because they'd be paying for exactly the speed they need. The point is, you should choose and pay for the speed that is right for your current usage patterns. You can always increase your speed level in the future, if needed.

Here are some usage guidelines to help you decide what speed is right for you:

  • 768 Kbps - This is great if you use the Internet for basic tasks such as checking your email and doing some light browsing.

  • 1.5 Mbps - This is great if you use the internet for basic tasks like emailing but also like to share photos online or make Skype for voice only calls.

  • 3-10 Mbps - These speeds are great if you use the internet for all the basic tasks such as emailing and online shopping but also like to stream TV shows, view YouTube videos, play online games or make video calls.

  • 15-25 Mbps - These speeds allow you to do all the things we have already mentioned plus support the use of multiple devices connected to the internet at the same time. If you play Xbox online in one room, and stream videos in another, these speeds may be for you.

Connection Speed is the speed at which information moves between your computer and the Internet. Information speeds are described in 2 ways, depending on the direction the information is moving. "Download speed" describes how quickly information moves from the Internet to your computer (for example, viewing a web page or receiving an e-mail). "Upload speed" describes how quickly information moves from your computer to the Internet (for example, sending an e-mail or posting a picture on a web site.)

Choose your modem and installation

 Help me choose

Help me choose

You have 2 options in your modem selection today - you can either lease or purchase your modem.


  • Lease
    Customers who lease their modem prefer not to have the upfront cost of purchasing equipment. A small monthly lease payment versus the one-time cost of buying a modem allows customers to take full advantage of our Internet technology while maintaining a low cost option. The modem lease option offers flexibility for advances in technology should you choose to upgrade your Internet service at any point in the future; you can simply swap your lease to a new modem.

  • Purchase
    Customers who purchase their modem prefer to pay for the modem cost up front and be done with it. They pay the one-time purchase fee and reduce their monthly bill over the long run. The risk for these customers is that they may need to purchase a new modem in the future if they upgrade their Internet service to a technology that requires a new modem type.

By leasing a CenturyLink provided modem/router you receive not only the modem/router, but also 24/7 technical support for the device, continual software updates to ensure the device works with your High-Speed Internet service, and our technicians will be able to help install and/or replace the device if there are ever any future issues with the device. None of these services will be available to you if you decide you would like to provide your own modem/router.

Calling Features

Here are just a few of the many features we offer:

Caller ID

Know who's calling before you pick up the phone by seeing their name and phone number on your display unit.

Call Waiting

When you're already on a call, a special tone alerts you of a new call. A simple push of a button lets you answer the new call while your other caller stays connected on a second line.

Call Waiting ID

Combines the benefits of Call Waiting and Caller ID.

Voice Mail

When you can't answer, automatically send callers to your personal voice mailbox, where they can leave a message. When you pick up your phone, a special "interrupted" dial tone lets you know you have new messages.

3-Way Calling

Connect with two different numbers at the same time, so you can have a group chat. It works with both local and long-distance calls.

Call Rejection

Block unwanted or annoying calls. Store up to 15 different numbers on your "rejection list", and when an unwanted caller tries to get through, a recorded message tells them you're not accepting calls.

Call Forwarding

Send all your calls to a different phone number, so you can be reached even when you're away from home.

Call Return

Dial *69 to find out who your last caller was and if you want, easily call them back. If the caller's line is busy, it'll continue redialing for up to 30 minutes and alert you with a special ring when the call can be completed.

Anonymous Call Rejection

Prevent calls from ringing on your phone if the caller's number is "private," "unknown," or "unavailable." A message tells the caller how to temporarily unblock their name and number if they want to reach you.

Selective Call Forwarding

Send the calls you want to a desired phone number, including your cell phone, answering system, pager or even an international number, and never miss an important call again.

Selective Call Rejection

Control your phone by blocking unwanted or annoying calls from certain numbers. When an unwanted caller tries to get through, a pre-recorded message says you're not accepting calls.

Speed Call

Dial Your Favorite Numbers - Calling your most important phone numbers can be as simple as dialing two digits. Program long-distance, local, international or emergency numbers for fast and easy access. Simply make a list of the 30 numbers you want to program and assign each one a two-digit code.

Busy Redial

Busy signals slowing you down? Just press *66 and automatically redial the number for up to 30 minutes.
